World Happiness Project

Introduction

The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey of the state of global happiness. The report continues to gain global recognition as governments, organizations and civil society increasingly use happiness indicators to inform their policy-making decisions. Leading experts across fields – economics, psychology, survey analysis, national statistics, health, public policy and more – describe how measurements of well-being can be used effectively to assess the progress of nations. The reports review the state of happiness in the world today and show how the new science of happiness explains personal and national variations in happiness.

Key Questions & Objectives

  1. What countries or regions rank the highest in overall happiness and each of the six factors contributing to happiness?
  2. How do life expectancy and access to healthcare correlate with happiness scores?
  3. Did any country experience a significant increase or decrease in happiness?
  4. Is there a correlation between perceived freedom to make life choices and happiness scores?
  5. How does GDP per capita correlate with happiness scores?

Dataset & Tools

The project leverages open-source datasets from Kaggle.The happiness scores and rankings use data from the Gallup World Poll. The scores are based on answers to the main life evaluation question asked in the poll.

The Tools used are - Anaconda, Juypter & Python
